Bounty Red-Flag Card

12 checks before you claim, code, spend, or share access

Free preflight

Mark a flag when it is present—or when authoritative evidence is missing. A low count means “continue checking,” not “safe.”

  1. Payout is not verifiably funded.A number in a title, issue, or comment is not proof of escrow or ability to pay.
  2. The claim window is closed or ambiguous.Issue status can differ from the platform slot, deadline, or eligibility window.
  3. Someone else is assigned or already building.Check claims, assignees, linked PRs, forks, comments, and submissions.
  4. Acceptance criteria are missing or subjective.“Improve,” “support,” and “production-ready” need testable definitions.
  5. The payer may lack acceptance authority.Confirm who can merge, approve, resolve disputes, and release funds.
  6. You must pay, bond, purchase, or provision first.Count subscriptions, cloud spend, devices, SIMs, domains, and bonds.
  7. Delivery requires private credentials or production access.A bounty is never authorization to access secrets, data, or live systems.
  8. Payment depends on third-party action.Upstream adoption, promotion, app review, or endorsement may be outside your control.
  9. The real scope exceeds the headline task.Include setup, tests, docs, deployment, review cycles, support, and collection.
  10. The project baseline is unhealthy or inactive.Archived code, broken tests, missing fixtures, or silence can block acceptance.
  11. Rights or compliance obligations are unclear.Verify licenses, data rights, disclosure, sanctions, taxes, and platform terms.
  12. Expected hourly return falls below your floor.Adjust for fees, revisions, communication, collection risk, and total time.

Hard stop until resolved

Unfunded payout, closed or expired claim, unauthorized access, prohibited conduct, or mandatory upfront spending you cannot independently justify.

(payout × payment probability − unavoidable costs) ÷ total hours

Count the flags

0–1Continue to full due diligence. Not a GO.
2–3Pause and verify with authoritative evidence.
4+Default to NO-GO unless evidence changes.
Evidence beats optimism. Unknown is not “safe.” Never fabricate demand, proof, eligibility, identity, or completion. Triage aid only—not professional advice.
